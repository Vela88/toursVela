Travel Blog Oaxaca

The most delicious state in Mexico

Oaxacan Chocolate at restaurant "Zapoteca Azuzena"

Check out this awsome restaurant. One of the best Oaxacan Chocolate ive had in the city!

Cathedral in Oaxaca

I had the good fortune to witness a projection lighting up the Cathedral.

Northern Sierra Mountains

Located 45 minutes from the city are various towns surrounded by tall pine trees. Bring a jacket because it get chilly up here.

Temple of Santo Domingo of Guzman

Santo Domingo is one of the most popular churches of Oaxaca. Known by the locals as "Santo"; it's a common place to meet up with friends, family, and tour guides.

Hierve el Agua

A beautiful destination to visit, especially if you enjoy the outdoors. These are actually mineral deposits that have been formed over a long period of time as a result of mineral-rich water cascading over the cliff's edge and trickling down the steep mountainside. Water pours down the rock wall, forming massive stalactites like those found in caves.

Calendas in Oaxaca

A Calenda is a tradition that dates back to the 16th Century; where locals and visitors are welcome to partake and parade around the city.
